Automated Pothole & Road Surface Condition Monitor

A classical computer vision-based system designed to detect road defects using homography, texture analysis, and contour-based severity grading — delivering scalable and cost-effective infrastructure monitoring.

Problem Statement

Road defects increase accident risks and cause billions in vehicle damage. Manual inspection is slow and unsafe, while LiDAR systems are expensive. We propose a scalable classical CV solution for automated monitoring.

System Pipeline

1. Image Acquisition

RGB camera mounted on vehicle captures continuous road imagery.

2. Preprocessing

Grayscale conversion, noise reduction, illumination normalization.

3. Geometric Correction

Homography transforms perspective view into bird’s-eye view.

4. Texture Extraction

Local Binary Patterns isolate surface roughness.

5. Segmentation

Morphological operations & contour detection localize defects.

6. Severity Grading

Damage ratio computed and priority level assigned.

Severity Classification

Minor: < 5% damage ratio

Moderate: 5–15% damage ratio

Severe: > 15% damage ratio

Technology Stack

Python
OpenCV
NumPy
scikit-image
